Features

Everything you need to message your workspace

DM as yourself

Messages arrive from your own Slack identity — not a faceless bot. Recipients see your name, avatar, and can reply directly to you.

Schedule for later

Pick an exact date and time for delivery. Messages land in the BullMQ queue and fire when the clock hits your chosen moment.

Filter and select

Narrow your recipient list with criteria-based filters before sending. Target the exact audience you need from your entire workspace.

Rate-limited by design

A Redis-backed BullMQ queue paces every message so you stay inside Slack’s API limits, even for large recipient lists.

OAuth and access control

Workspace OAuth handles authentication. An access-control layer with platform-based handling gates who can send and what they can send.

Bulk delivery

Compose one message, deliver it to hundreds. The queue handles concurrency and retries so nothing falls through the cracks.

How it works

From message to delivered, in six steps

1

Send a message to the bot

Write the message you want to broadcast and send it to DM-Sender in Slack. The bot records it.

2

Bot replies with options

DM-Sender replies in a thread confirming it recorded your message, with two buttons: Send Now or Send Later.

3

Choose audience and options

A modal opens where you select recipients — all users, specific channels, or individual users. Filter by active/inactive status and toggle link unfurling.

4

Schedule or send immediately

Send Now fires right away. Send Later adds date, time, and timezone pickers so you control exactly when it lands.

5

Messages enter the queue

BullMQ enqueues each DM with rate-limiting and retry logic, staying within Slack’s API caps.

6

Report delivered in thread

Once all DMs are sent, the bot posts a report in the same thread — total sent, successful, failed, and recipient list.
